# Arkansas Medical Marijuana Amendment of 2024

## What This Amendment Does (In Plain English)

This is a proposed change to the Arkansas Constitution. It builds on the **2016 Medical Marijuana Amendment** (known as Amendment 98). It makes several big changes to how medical marijuana works in the state.

---

## Key Changes

### 1. More Medical Conditions Now Qualify

- **Before:** Only a specific list of medical conditions allowed you to get a medical marijuana card.
- **Now:** Any condition that a healthcare professional believes is **debilitating** (seriously limiting to your daily life) and **might be helped by medical marijuana** can qualify — even if it's not on the original list.

---

### 2. Patients Can Grow Marijuana Plants at Home

- **Who can grow:** Qualifying patients or their caregivers, as long as they are **at least 21 years old**.
- **Where:** Only at their **home** (their domicile).
- **How much:** A **limited number of plants** of **limited sizes** (specific limits to be set by regulators).
- **What you can't do:** You **cannot sell, trade, or barter** any plants or marijuana you grow at home.
- **Who regulates this:** The **Alcoholic Beverage Control Division** will oversee and regulate home growing.

---

### 3. Adult Possession and Sales — But Only If Federal Law Changes

- **Adults could possess** up to **one ounce** of usable marijuana.
- **Sales to adults** would be allowed — **but only if** the federal government removes marijuana from its list of controlled substances first.
- Until that federal change happens, this part does **not** go into effect.

---

### 4. More Types of Healthcare Professionals Can Approve Patients

- **Before:** Only certain physicians (doctors) could certify patients for medical marijuana.
- **Now:** The following professionals can certify patients, as long as they hold a **valid, unrestricted Arkansas license**:
  - **Doctors of Medicine (MDs)**
  - **Doctors of Osteopathic Medicine (DOs)**
  - **Nurse Practitioners**
  - **Physician's Assistants**
  - **Pharmacists**

---

### 5. Fewer Barriers for Healthcare Professionals

- **Removed requirement:** Doctors no longer need to have a **controlled substance license on file with the DEA** (Drug Enforcement Agency) to certify patients.
- **Removed requirement:** Doctors no longer need to have a formal **"physician-patient relationship"** with someone seeking a medical marijuana card. This means:
  - You **don't** have to be an existing patient of that doctor.
  - Certification can happen **in person or by telemedicine** without a prior relationship.

---

### 6. Sections of the Old Amendment Are Deleted

- **Sections 23 and 26** of Amendment 98 are **fully repealed** (completely removed). These sections are eliminated in their entirety.

---

### 7. The Original 2016 Amendment Is Affirmed

- This amendment **confirms and supports** the original 2016 Medical Marijuana Amendment and any changes the state legislature has already made to it. In other words, everything that was already in place stays valid unless this new amendment specifically changes it.

---

## Bottom Line

This amendment **expands** who can use medical marijuana, **who can approve** it, **what conditions qualify**, and adds the ability to **grow plants at home**. It also sets up a path for adult possession and sales — but **only if the federal government legalizes marijuana first**.
