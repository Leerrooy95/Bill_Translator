Arkansas Medical Marijuana Amendment of 2024

What This Amendment Does:

This amendment changes the Arkansas Constitution to expand the state's medical marijuana program. It builds on the original 2016 Medical Marijuana Amendment (Amendment 98). It also confirms that the 2016 amendment and any laws that changed it are still valid.

Here is what changes:

1. More Medical Conditions Now Qualify

Right now, only certain listed conditions let you get a medical marijuana card. Under this amendment, a health care provider can also approve you if they believe your condition is serious enough that marijuana might help — even if your condition is not on the original list.

2. Growing Marijuana Plants at Home

Patients who qualify, or their caregivers, may now grow a limited number of marijuana plants at home. You must be at least 21 years old to do this. The plants must be limited in number and size. You may only grow them at the place where you live. The plants must be only for the personal use of a qualifying patient. You may not sell, trade, or barter any plants you grow at home. The Alcoholic Beverage Control Division will set the rules for home growing.

3. Adult Possession and Future Sales

Adults may carry up to one ounce of usable marijuana. If the federal government removes marijuana from its list of controlled substances, then stores may sell marijuana to adults.

4. Sections Removed

Sections 23 and 26 of Amendment 98 are fully repealed. They no longer apply.

5. More Types of Health Care Providers Can Certify Patients

Before, only certain doctors could sign off on a patient's marijuana card. Now, the following providers can certify patients, as long as they hold a valid, unrestricted license to practice in Arkansas:
- Doctors of medicine
- Doctors of osteopathic medicine
- Nurse practitioners
- Physician's assistants
- Pharmacists

6. Fewer Requirements for Providers

Providers no longer need a controlled substance license on file with the U.S. Drug Enforcement Agency to certify patients. Providers also no longer need to have an existing in-person or telemedicine relationship with the person seeking a medical marijuana card.
