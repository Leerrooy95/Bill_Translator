# Arkansas Medical Marijuana Amendment of 2024

## Plain-English Translation

This is a proposed change to the Arkansas Constitution. It builds on the state's existing medical marijuana law (Amendment 98, passed in 2016). Here's what it does:

---

## 1. More Medical Conditions Would Qualify

- **Current law** lists specific medical conditions that allow a patient to use medical marijuana.
- **This amendment** expands that list. It would let a healthcare provider approve **any condition** they believe is seriously affecting a patient's quality of life — as long as they think medical marijuana could help.

---

## 2. Patients Could Grow Marijuana Plants at Home

- **Qualifying patients** (or their caregivers) who are **at least 21 years old** could grow a limited number of marijuana plants at home.
- The plants must be **only for the patient's personal medical use**.
- **You could NOT sell, trade, or barter** any of the plants or marijuana you grow.
- The **Alcoholic Beverage Control Division** would be in charge of making rules about home growing.

---

## 3. Adults Could Possess Small Amounts — With a Big "If"

- Adults could possess **up to one ounce** of usable marijuana.
- Marijuana could also be **sold to adults** — **but only if** the federal government first removes marijuana from its list of controlled substances.
- In other words, this part **only kicks in if federal law changes first**.

---

## 4. Two Existing Sections of the Law Would Be Removed

- **Sections 23 and 26** of Amendment 98 would be **completely repealed** (deleted from the law).

---

## 5. More Types of Healthcare Providers Could Certify Patients

Under current law, only certain doctors can approve a patient for a medical marijuana card. This amendment **expands who can certify patients** to include:

- **Doctors of medicine** (MDs)
- **Doctors of osteopathic medicine** (DOs)
- **Nurse practitioners**
- **Physician's assistants**
- **Pharmacists**

All of these providers must hold a **valid, unrestricted license** to practice in Arkansas.

---

## 6. Fewer Hoops for Providers to Jump Through

- Doctors would **no longer need** a controlled substance license on file with the **U.S. Drug Enforcement Agency (DEA)** to certify patients.
- Providers would **no longer need** to have an established "physician-patient relationship" with someone seeking a medical marijuana card.
- This means certifications could happen **more easily**, including through **telemedicine** (video or phone visits).

---

## 7. The Existing 2016 Amendment Is Confirmed

- This amendment **officially affirms** that the original 2016 medical marijuana amendment — and any changes the legislature has already made to it — are valid and in effect.
